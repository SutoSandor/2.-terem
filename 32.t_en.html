Recreational Hospital in the castle 1914-1918


Even before the First World War, - in the 1849 Hungarian Revolution and in 1866, during the Austro-Prussian War - a temporary hospital was created in the castle. At the beginning of the Great War, on the regulation of Franz Joseph a so-called Recreational Hospital opened in the royal castle, which operated until the end of the war.

No major surgery was carried out in the Recreational Hospital, besides minor interventions the soldiers were brought here mainly for post-treatment. The furnishing met the highest requirements of the time.

The hospital provided care for 40 officers and 60 privates. The length of stay was determined by the army. Two doctors served here for a longer period: Dr. Lajos Teghze and Dr. Kálmán Csengeri. The operation of the hospital was directed by Dr. Aladár Szegedi-Maszák court counselor, and was supervised by the respective chief court officer. The patients were cared for by the Hospitaller Sisters, they also carried out kitchen duty. Members of the royal family repeatedly visited the convalescing soldiers here, on major holidays they also gave gifts.

The castle hospital was emptied in August 1918, because they began the renovation work of the building, so the ruler (Charles IV.) could move there with his family.
