Sándor Petőfi and the Petőfi chest


Petőfi Sándor spent longer time in Gödöllő twice. First in 1843, when he translated Robin Hood. He lived in the Szombath family's serf house. He rented a room from the widowed Jánosné Szombath, and worked on the translation for three weeks. From the equipment of the room a painted Komarno chest, which according to local legend held Petőfi's clothes and manuscripts, became the property of the museum. Among the population of Gödöllő the furniture is called Petőfi-chest, although during the restoration a painted date of 1860 was found, according to which the poet could never store his belongings in it. However, the chest remained an important piece of the local cult of '48.

In 1845, during his second stay János Erdélyi Reformed pastor hosted Petőfi. Here he met Berta Mednyánszky, the daughter of the steward of the Grassalkovich estate in Gödöllő. The poet wrote his cycle of poems entitled Pearls of Love to her.
